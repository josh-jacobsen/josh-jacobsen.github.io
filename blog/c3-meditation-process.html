C3: Meditation and Process

How did the 'process over product' concept affect the way you tackled the site redesign and rebuild?

I'm not going to lie, I had significant difficulties with the process over product approach this week. I like to explore subjects in depth and I often find myself asking "why" a lot and wanting to know more about paticular points within a topic. This approach posed several problems to me this week, as process over product in my case meant just accepting some things as a given so I could move on to developing the other aspects of my site.

The best example of this was when I was creating the navigation menu for my site. From my wireframing and looking at other sites for inspiration, I have a really good idea of what I want to achieve. Having gotten 80% of the way there, I then spent a significant ammount of time trying to get the rest of the 20% compelted, instead of acknowleging that the nav menu was "fit for purpose" in it's current state and carparking the issue for later development once I had completed all the other tasks for the week (such as the blog posts and reflections).

What did you think about mediation before reading chapter 2?

I guess that I was under the assumption that mediation was something that takes a lot of time to do properly (as in a one hour mediation session as a minimum), and that having your attention wander during a meditation session is by definition a negative thing.

What new things have you learnt about mediation?

I like the way in which Meng described the process of recapturing lost attention as a way of strengthing your brain and meta-attention. This is a different way of looking at one of the biggest issues that I have faced in the past with maintaining attention (it often feels like I can't focus on my breathing, or whatever it happens to be that I am trying to focus on). Now when this happens I look at it as an opportunity to develop a weakness, instead of castigating myself for having let it happen.

Did any of the suggested mediation techniques stand out to you?

Coming up with my position for meditating is a great idea. Thinking of whatever position that I end up adopting as having to be one in which I feel "majestic, dignified, and awe-inspiring" gives me the abilty to play around with a number of options while ensuring that I have a goal in mind while doing so. So far I have found the best position for me to be on a bean bag in the middle of the living room floor.

Any other musings?

Going back to BJ Fogg's teachings on developing habits by linking them to existing behaviours, I'm going to aim to do 2 minutes of meditation every time that I put the kettle on during the working day. Keeping it at 2 minutes is easily achieveable (the 10 minutes that I did while reading chapter 2 of Search Inside Yourself was getting to be on the long side for my current state of meditation-realted ability), and putting on the kettle has become a regualr habit while working from home, so if I can get some additional benefit out of the activity then so much the better.
